# CFB-GM benchmark baseline — 2026-07-13

The first full policy benchmark of the shipped v1.4 game (commit ~9e2b2f6):
**104 headless dynasties, 3,120 simulated seasons** — 4 scripted decision
profiles × 3 prestige tiers × 8 seeds × 30 seasons, plus a Brutal-difficulty
arm. Raw per-season data: [`2026-07-13-baseline.results.json`](2026-07-13-baseline.results.json).

Re-run after any tuning change and diff against this file:

```
npm run bench:gm -- run 0 20      # …repeat in slices to 104 (~10s/run)
npm run bench:gm -- report        # aggregate .bench/
```

## Method

- **Policies act only on user-visible information** (stars, fuzzy OVR bands,
  locks, leads, asks — never hidden ovr/dev/ceiling):
  - `autopilot` — zero decisions (a player who ignores every system)
  - `recruiter` — spends all 600 RAP weekly across 12 targets; no retention, no portal
  - `portal-gm` — no recruiting; pays every affordable retention ask, bids 95% of budget in the portal
  - `balanced` — recruits 6 targets, retains 80+ OVR, bids 60% of budget
- **Programs** (by baked prestige): Georgia (6★ blue blood), Arizona State (3★ mid), Arkansas (1★ bottom)
- Seeds 9001-9008; 30 seasons each; scripted profiles are a **floor** on managed play, not a human ceiling. Cells are n=8 — treat CFP-rate deltas under ~8pts as noise.

## Findings

1. **Retention is the load-bearing mechanic.** Unmanaged teams lose ~16
   players/season to the portal while AI programs pay to keep their stars.
   The recruiter profile signs #1-2 classes for 30 straight years and still
   decays to ~5.5 wins at Georgia — it develops talent and donates it.
   portal-gm (never recruits) beats pure recruiting at every tier.
2. **Agency is worth ~+3 wins/yr** late-game at Georgia (balanced 7.0 vs
   autopilot 4.0) and +2.4 at Arizona State. Engagement pays as designed.
3. **Neglect spirals hard**: an unmanaged blue blood ends 30 years at
   prestige ~1.6 with a 63-OVR roster.
4. **The bottom-tier climb is likely too bleak**: 30 years of competent
   scripted play at Arkansas rarely reaches the CFP (1-2 runs of 8).
   Chicken-and-egg: +1 prestige needs 10 wins, near-impossible at 1★.
5. **Active recruiting may be too dominant**: any engaged profile lands
   top-2 classes at a blue blood and top-13 at Arkansas; the top-15 class
   mandate is nearly free.
6. **Brutal difficulty is statistically invisible at n=8** — its NIL/RAP
   squeeze isn't where games are decided (see #1); it should probably also
   sharpen AI retention/portal aggression.

## Tuning candidates for the next pass

- Soften the low-prestige ladder (e.g., +1 prestige at 9 wins for ≤2★).
- Heat up AI pursuit of 4-5★ recruits so #1 classes cost real focus.
- Make difficulty scale AI retention/portal behavior, not just budgets.
- Re-check the ~16/season unmanaged portal outflow in real play.
