# CFB-GM benchmark baseline — 2026-07-15 (post-ADR-0027 mechanical rework)

The policy grid re-run after the ADR-0027 rework (offseason inversion, shared
stamina pool, fit-priced 5-round portal, tier churn, schemes, 5-role staff):
**104 headless dynasties, 3,120 simulated seasons** — same grid as the
[2026-07-13 baseline](2026-07-13-baseline.md). Raw per-season data:
[`2026-07-15-post-adr-0027.results.json`](2026-07-15-post-adr-0027.results.json).
This file is the new diff target after any tuning change.

## Method deltas vs 2026-07-13

- Policies were ported to the offseason-week API: recruiting spends the
  ~100/week shared stamina pool across the 8 offseason weeks (visit and HC
  in-home are once-per-recruit); retention answers week 2; portal offers are
  priced off the visible fit discount (`effectiveAsk`) each of rounds 1-5.
- Everything else (policies' information diet, programs, seeds, horizons) is
  unchanged. Cells are n=8 — treat CFP-rate deltas under ~8pts as noise.

## Headline movements vs the old game

- **The portal is now the dominant lever** — by design ("the portal is a
  battleground"): portal-gm at a blue blood sustains ~10+ w/yr with 16
  natties/240 seasons and ends at 6★ prestige; ignoring the portal
  (recruiter) leaks ~25 players/yr unmanaged (was ~16) and no longer
  converts good classes into wins at low prestige.
- **Recruiting is calibrated inside prestige gravity**: the visit-spam meta
  the first post-rework grid exposed (class #1 every year from a 1★ program)
  is gone with the one-visit rule — an all-in recruiter lands ~#24 at 1★,
  ~#4-7 at 6★ (the old baseline's ~#1.3-from-anywhere was the broken shape).
- **Engagement beats autopilot everywhere**: at the bottom tier, balanced
  play reaches 9 wins in 4/8 runs vs autopilot's 1/8; at the top, unmanaged
  Georgia decays (9.5 → 6.9 w/yr) while any engaged policy sustains.
- **Difficulty arm behaves**: Brutal ≈ Normal for balanced@mid within noise
  (the AI sharpening and budget squeeze offset the +1-win mandate pressure).
